Hacking Phases

As there are steps to every hackers do follow some predefined rules or steps to hack into the system.

1. Information Gathering:

Information gathering refers to preparatory phase where in security asssessment is focused on collecting as much information as possible about a target an attacker looks for gathering information about target to launching an attack

There are two type of Information gathering Passive information gathering involves gathering information without directly interacting with the target

For Example: searching on news or Google group

Active Information gathering involves interacting with the traget directly by any means.

For example: Facebook Chat or call

2. Scanning:

Scanning refers to the second phase when the attacker scans the network for specific information on network and attacker extract information such as like machines port, OS details, service etc. to launch attack

3. Gaining Access:

Gaining access the point where the attacker access to system or network. The attacker can escalate privilege to obtain complete control of system in the process, intermediate system that are connected to it are also compromised.

4. Maintaing Access:

Once a hacker has gained access, by hacking the system they want to keep that access for future exploitation and attacks. They use backdoors, rootkits, or Trojans for future access to compromise system.

5. Clearing Tracks:

Once hackers have been able to gain and maintain access, they always try to cover their tracks to avoid detection by computer forensic expert. Hackers try to remove all traces of the attack, such as log files or intrusion detection system alarms.

What is IP Address

An IP address is like a special number that helps your computer or phone know how to talk to other computers or phones on the internet. Just like how you have a name that helps your friends and family know who you are, every computer or phone connected to the internet has a unique IP address that helps it communicate with other devices.

Eg: 192.158.1.38

I'll explain a bit more!

An IP address is made up of a series of numbers separated by dots, like 123.45.67.89. This number is assigned to your device by your internet service provider (ISP), and it allows your device to send and receive information over the internet.

Think of it like a phone number for your device. Just like you can call someone by dialing their phone number, your device can communicate with other devices on the internet by using their IP address.

Here are some things you can do with an IP address:

  1. Browse the web: When you type a website address into your browser, your device uses the website's IP address to connect to it and display the webpage.
  2. Send and receive emails: Your email service uses your IP address to send and receive emails.
  3. Play online games: Online games use IP addresses to connect players from different locations and enable them to play games together
  4. Make video calls: Video conferencing applications use IP addresses to connect users and enable them to communicate through video calls.
  5. Download and upload files: File sharing applications use IP addresses to connect users and enable them to download and upload files.

Overall, an IP address is a crucial component of internet connectivity and enables us to perform a variety of online activities.
